Service

Service matters to me, personally and professionally.

AmeriCorps

After completing my undergraduate studies in 2014, I served for a year in AmeriCorps as a College Completion Coach. My challenge: help mentor a cohort of at-risk students attending a community college in Cleveland, Ohio. Getting to know my students, watching them dream, struggle, and strive toward a new life, left an indelible mark on me. Too few of them passed remedial algebra and basic writing, let alone graduated. If a nations education system can be judeged by how it serves the least among us, then we are not doing enough for vulnerable students like those I met in Cleveland. I am convinced that better information capacities, particularly among public, open-access colleges, is at least one piece of the puzzle of quality, accessible post-secondary education.

Serving God, Serving People

As a follower of Christ, I know my purpose in this life is to serve God and other people. This conviction motivates my interest in education and in open-access, public colleges in particular. These institutions serve a crucial role in our society, yet receive too little support to do their work effectively. I want to be part of a movement to improve public higher education as a way serve people and cultivate their God-given potential.
