Lost opportunities, hidden liabilities

For many colleges, inadequate practices in information collection, management, and analysis hamper their ability to fulfill their missions.

Collection

Too few colleges are intentional and consistent about what information they collect about students and how they collect it. Such poor policies can harm decision-making and reporting because of haphazard, inadequate data.

Management

Even if collegs collect the right types of information at the right points in time, if they are not thoughtful about how they organize, store, and update records, good collection policies will be for naught.

Analysis

Institutional researchers too often lack basic skills in data analysis. Some of the most pressing questions that colleges as of their data -- retention prediction, enrollment forecasting, evaluation of interventions -- require better statistical training.
