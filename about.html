Better information, better education

College tuition is rising. Students and parents question the accessibility of a degree. Faculty and administrators worry about college quality. How can better institutional information capacities help each of these problems?

Education

I hold a Bachelor of Arts in History (summa cum laude; Phi Beta Kappa) from Gettysburg College. Currently, I am pursuing a Master of Arts in Higher Education Institutional Research and a Master of Science in Information, both at the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor.

Professional Experience

My professional experiences thus far have focused on community colleges and reflect my particular interest in public, open-access institutions. Before beginning graduate school, I served as an advisor for a cohort of at-risk students at Lakeland Community College, Cleveland, Ohio. I currently work part-time at Jackson College, a small, rural two-year institution in Jackson, Michigan.

Hobbies

I ride a road bike. Sometimes fast. Sometimes less so. But regardless, I love to ride. But that's fine with me, because I also love to read about really anything in the humantities and social sciences, and I don't need any hills to do that.
